Pricing

Firstly, offering our web application for free can attract a wider audience. People are more likely to try out a product that doesn't come with a cost attached to it, especially if they are unsure about its quality or usefulness. By offering our app for free, we can potentially reach a larger audience and increase our user base. This can lead to more feedback, suggestions, and bug reports, which can ultimately help us improve our product.

Another advantage of offering a free service is that it eliminates the risk of customers being dissatisfied with the product and wanting a refund. By not charging anything, we eliminate the need for a refund policy and any associated costs. This also puts less pressure on our customer support team, as they won't have to deal with refund requests and complaints related to pricing.

Furthermore, by offering our web application for free, we can build goodwill and trust with our customers. They are more likely to recommend our product to others if they are satisfied with its quality and don't feel like they've been taken advantage of. This can lead to positive word-of-mouth marketing and organic growth of our user base.

Lastly, while we are offering our product for free, we can still make money through other means such as advertising, partnerships, or by offering premium features for a fee. By having a larger user base, we increase the potential for these other revenue streams to be successful. For example, we can offer companies the opportunity to advertise their products to our users, or we can offer advanced features for a fee, which may be desirable to some users.

In conclusion, the decision to offer our web application for free can potentially bring numerous benefits to our business. By attracting a wider audience, eliminating the need for a refund policy, building trust and goodwill, and having the potential to make money through other means, we can establish ourselves in the market and potentially grow our business in the long term.
